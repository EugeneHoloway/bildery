Executive Summary

Liki24.de currently lacks any subscription or replenishment mechanism for recurring pet supply purchases. Customers who reorder the same SKUs every 4–8 weeks do so entirely manually — renavigating, re-searching, and re-adding to cart each cycle. This creates unnecessary churn exposure in a category defined by high predictability and switching costs that should work in our favour.

Autoship & Save is an opt-in scheduled-delivery subscription with a tiered price discount (5–10%) in exchange for a committed purchase cadence. Based on cohort analysis and published analogues from Zooplus DE, Chewy US, and Amazon Subscribe & Save, we project +$103.89 incremental LTV per activated subscriber and first-year revenue of $19.8K–$33K at base/bull activation rates against the eligible segment.

Decision
Approved for A/B testing in Q2 2025. Scope limited to DE market, web + app surfaces, consumable SKUs (food, supplements, grooming). Full rollout is gated on the experiment reaching North Star thresholds defined in §05.

Problem Statement

Pet supply is a high-frequency, high-predictability category. A customer with a 10 kg labrador reorders the same 15 kg food bag roughly every 5–6 weeks — not because they want to re-evaluate, but because the bag runs out. Today, Liki24.de forces that customer to re-navigate, re-search, and re-add to cart every single time. This is both a friction problem and a missed lock-in opportunity:

ProblemBusiness ImpactCustomer Impact
No lock-in mechanism on repeat purchases ~18% of repeat buyers lapse annually to Zooplus or Amazon No incentive to consolidate pet spend on one platform
Manual reorder friction Competitors win reorders via 1-click subscribe with confirmed stock Time cost every 4–6 weeks to re-navigate and re-purchase
LTV ceiling without retention Average LTV capped at 4–5 purchase cycles before churn Loses access to loyalty pricing available on competing platforms
Why not solve with UX improvements alone?
Saved carts, one-click reorder, and reorder reminders were evaluated. They reduce friction but do not create a commitment mechanism. Only a subscription with a discount generates mutual lock-in and the demand predictability needed for supply chain optimisation. Both effects are required.

04

Proposed Solution

Autoship & Save is a scheduled-delivery subscription for consumable SKUs with a tiered price discount in exchange for a committed replenishment cadence. The customer sets a frequency (2–12 weeks), selects a discount tier, and receives automatic orders. Skip, pause, and cancel are fully self-serve — no contact required, no penalty.

FeatureSpecificationPriority
Subscription enrollment PDP toggle "Subscribe & Save" + standalone subscription manager P0
Delivery frequency selector 2 / 4 / 6 / 8 / 10 / 12 week intervals; ML-suggested default from purchase history P0
Discount tiers 5% (4w+ cadence), 7% (6w+ cadence), 10% first order always P0
Skip / pause / cancel Fully self-serve via dashboard; no friction, no retention interstitial, no contact required P0
D-5 pre-shipment email Reminder with tokenised skip CTA (no login required); add-to-order upsell included P1
Smart cadence suggestions ML-inferred interval based on prior purchase frequency shown as default option P2

06

UX Flow

The primary enrollment path is on the Product Detail Page. The design principle: zero added friction for customers who don't want to subscribe; two additional taps for those who do. Everything after enrollment is self-serve.

1
Subscribe toggle on PDP
Eligible SKUs show a "One-time / Subscribe & Save" radio selector below the Add to Cart CTA. Default is one-time. Selecting subscribe reveals the frequency picker and live price comparison showing savings at each interval.
2
Frequency selection
Customer selects delivery interval (2–12 weeks). The system pre-selects the statistically inferred interval from prior purchase history where available. Applied discount updates in real time.
3
Checkout confirmation
Cart and checkout show a subscription summary: next delivery date, applied discount, and a one-line reminder that the subscription can be paused or cancelled at any time with no penalty.
4
Post-purchase confirmation email
Confirmation includes the subscription dashboard link, next order date, and a tokenised "Skip next delivery" CTA (no login required). Designed to set expectations and eliminate support contacts from users who forgot they subscribed.
5
D-5 pre-shipment reminder
Email sent 5 days before scheduled shipment. Allows one-click skip without cancellation. Includes an "Add to this order" upsell for related consumables (treats, flea treatment, etc.).
6
Self-serve subscription management
Account dashboard: view active subscriptions, edit frequency, skip, pause (up to 60 days), add/remove SKUs, cancel. Cancellation requires a single confirmation step only. No retention interstitials, no support contact required.
Edge Cases & Handling
ScenarioHandling
SKU out of stock on scheduled delivery date Push delivery up to 5 days; notify customer D-7 with option to swap SKU or skip
Customer updates delivery address Next scheduled delivery uses updated address; previous in-progress deliveries unaffected
Payment method expires before renewal Email 14d prior; retry D-3 and D-0; pause subscription (not cancel) after 3 consecutive failures
Price increase on a subscribed SKU Email 14d prior with option to cancel at zero penalty before the new price takes effect
